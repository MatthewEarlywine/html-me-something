My Favorite Books

For as long as I can remember, I have loved to read. Mysteries, adventures, mythologies, histories, you name it and I just ate it up. So, with books being such a large part of my life, I'd like to share some of my personal favorite books with you.

  • After Man: A Zoology of the Future

    • By: Dougal Dixon



    This text presents possible outcomes on the development of life on Earth one million years after mankind has gone extinct. It extrapolates on evolutionary trends and hypothesizes on how certain ecological niches will be filled when humans and the species we either directly or indirectly control (such as cattle, horses, wolves and deer) have vanished. Not only does Dixon present an interesting and reasonably scientifically-grounded argument, but he also provides some extremely well-done illustrations of his various thought-experiment species.

  • Norse Mythology

    • By: Neil Gaiman



    A recent addition to my library, acclaimed author Neil Gaiman takes the wit and style that made him famous and applies them to the myths of the ancient Norse. These millenia-old tales have new life breathed into them, a sense of immediacy in Gaiman's short story-style not present in older, more academic retellings. While sadly not in an illustrated format, the vivaciousness of Gaiman's prose can very well make you think otherwise.
